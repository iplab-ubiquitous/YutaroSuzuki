Identifying Hand Gestures and Touch Positions on the Forearm through Electrical Impedance Method

Touch position identification

Hand gesture recognition

Publication

  • 2019 IEEE International Conference on Pervasive Computing and Communications Workshops (PerCom Workshops). Kyoto. March, 2019.

Related work in IPLAB

Background

　The opoerations, for example character input, to small wearable devices such as smart watches are performed by touch input to the on-board touch screen. However, it is difficult for users to input by touching, since the objects in the screen become smaller. This is caused the fat finger problems that it is difficult for users to push apart objects smaller than user's finger. In order to solve those problems, human skins are thought as touch interfaces.

Purpose

  • Realizing input to the small wearable devices by touching the forearm and hand gestures
  • Identifying hand gestures and touch positions on the forearm
  • Using easily attachable sensors

Approach

  • Attaching the electrodes to surround the forearm
  • Measuring the voltages on each electrode based on the electrical impedance method
  • Identifying touch positions and hand gestures by machine learning technique

　The electrical impedance method is a technique of estimating the internal structure by utilizing the difference in electrical conductivity. Installing electrodes arround the target area, applying AC to a pair of adjacent electrodes, and measuring voltages at all of the other electrodes in turn. We can claasify touch positoins and hand gestures by machine learning technique since the measured voltages change caused by a shunt current based on touch events, or transformation of the internal stracture of the forearm based on hand gestures.
